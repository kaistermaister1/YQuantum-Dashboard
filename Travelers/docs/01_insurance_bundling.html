The Goal: Choose which coverages go into which packages to maximize total expected profit.    The Catch: More discount = more customers, but less revenue per customer.
Optimization Lens: What Is the Solver Choosing?

Read the Decision Variables

xi,m = 1 means coverage i is included in package m.
xi,m = 0 means that coverage is left out of that package.
The optimization goal is to maximize total expected contribution across all coverage-package assignments.
A solution is feasible only if it obeys all family, dependency, incompatibility, and capacity rules.

Feasible vs. Infeasible Bundles

Feasible: enhanced auto liability + homeowners + collision + personal umbrella. This respects mandatory-family, dependency, and at-most-one rules.
Infeasible: renters + flood insurance. Flood requires homeowners, so this bundle breaks a dependency rule.
Also infeasible: gap insurance + personal property floater. That pair is explicitly incompatible.
The package cards on the right are candidate bundles to inspect, not certified optimal answers.

Profit Contribution Formula

ci,m = price × margin × (1 - discount)
      × (take_rate × affinity × (1 + 1.2 × discount))
1. price × margin × (1-discount) = unit margin after the package discount
2. take_rate × affinity × (1+1.2×discount) = stimulated take-rate factor
3. expected contribution = (unit margin) × (stimulated take-rate factor)
The discount simultaneously decreases revenue per unit and increases the demand factor — creating a non-trivial optimization landscape.
At 15% discount, expected profit per unit is $139.81. This slider shows the local tradeoff for one coverage in one package; the full optimizer must balance all coverages and all constraints at once.
