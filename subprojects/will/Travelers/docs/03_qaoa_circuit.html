Key Insight

QAOA is variational: a classical optimizer tunes quantum circuit parameters in a feedback loop.

Think of it like black-box parameter tuning: the optimizer proposes angles, the quantum computer estimates the cost, and the loop repeats.

min_{gamma,beta} <psi(gamma,beta)| H_cost |psi(gamma,beta)>

Important distinction: QAOA minimizes an expected cost, but the candidate answers are the measured bitstrings. Low expectation value usually helps, but the final answer still comes from sampling.

The quantum computer explores many states in superposition; the classical optimizer only sees noisy estimates of their average cost and steers the angles from there.

How To Read This Circuit

The diagram is symbolic: it shows how the QUBO terms map into quantum operations. Single-variable terms become one-qubit phase rotations; pairwise terms become ZZ couplings. Treat the exact API parameterization as platform-specific and follow the current Quantinuum tooling docs used at the hackathon.

Cost Layer Expanded: Encoding the Problem X

The cost layer translates the QUBO objective into quantum gates. For each qubit (coverage), and each pair of qubits (constraint), we apply rotations proportional to their coefficients.

Rz(gamma * c_i) per qubit
ZZPhase(gamma * lambda * w_ij) per pair
QUBO term What it means Circuit picture
x_i Reward or penalize selecting one coverage One-qubit Rz phase rotation
x_i x_j Couple two selections through a constraint or interaction Two-qubit ZZPhase interaction

Rz gates encode each coverage's profit contribution. The rotation angle is proportional to the coverage's objective coefficient c_i.

ZZPhase gates encode constraint penalties between coverage pairs. The rotation angle depends on the penalty weight w_ij and penalty strength lambda.

Notation note: The mathematical object is still a ZZ interaction, but the exact gate-call syntax depends on the current Quantinuum software stack. Use the challenge's live platform documentation for the precise API.

ZZPhase is a native two-qubit gate here, so this style of cost layer maps especially naturally to the trapped-ion gate set.
Mixer Layer Expanded: Exploring Solutions X

The mixer layer applies Rx(2*beta) rotations to every qubit. This rotates each qubit's state, allowing the quantum state to explore new candidate solutions.

Rx(2*beta) per qubit

Without the mixer, the circuit would stay stuck in the initial superposition. The mixer "stirs" the quantum state, letting probability amplitude flow between different bitstrings (candidate solutions).

This explainer uses the standard X-mixer. It does not preserve hard feasibility during the circuit, so infeasible bitstrings can still appear in the state. In this challenge, feasibility is encouraged mainly through penalty terms in the cost layer.

The balance between cost layer (exploitation) and mixer layer (exploration) is controlled by the angles gamma and beta -- this is what the classical optimizer tunes.

The Depth-Quality Tradeoff

The Tradeoff

More layers (higher p) can improve the best reachable objective, but they also cost more:

-- More parameters for the optimizer to tune (harder optimization)
-- Deeper circuit = more gates = more noise on real hardware
-- Diminishing returns: going from p=1 to p=2 helps a lot, p=10 to p=11 barely matters

For the challenge's package-local blocks, p=1 or p=2 is a practical starting point on near-term hardware, not a guaranteed optimum.

What Am I Looking At?

This is a synthetic 2D contour plot of the expected energy as a function of the two QAOA parameters (gamma and beta) for p = 1.

Dark blue = low energy (good solutions). Yellow/red = high energy (bad solutions).

The white dots trace the optimizer's path from start to convergence. For larger p, the true landscape lives in 2p dimensions, not just two.

Local Minima = Traps

The landscape has multiple valleys. A local search method can get stuck in a nearby minimum instead of finding the global best one.

Watch out for local minima! Multi-start strategies or better optimizers (COBYLA, SPSA) can help escape traps.
